Prof. Wole Soyinka

The man who first won the Nobel prize in sub-saharan african.

Here's the record of Prof. Wole Soyinka

  • 1934 - Born in Abeokuta, Ogun state.

  • 1954 - He continued at the University of Leeds.

  • 1973 - He took his doctorate.

  • 1958-1959 - During the six years spent in England, he was a dramaturgist at the Royal Court Theatre in London.

  • 1960 - He was awarded a Rockefeller bursary and returned to Nigeria to study African drama. At the same time, he taught drama and literature at various universities in Ibadan, Lagos, and Ife, where, since 1975, he has been professor of comparative literature.

  • 1960 - He founded the theatre group, “The 1960 Masks” and in 1964, the “Orisun Theatre Company”, in which he has produced his own plays and taken part as actor. He has periodically been visiting professor at the universities of Cambridge, Sheffield, and Yale.

  • 1967 - During the civil war in Nigeria, Soyinka appealed in an article for cease-fire. For this he was arrested, accused of conspiring with the Biafra rebels, and was held as a political prisoner for 22 months until 1969.

  • He is age 86 year now.
